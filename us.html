Between Us

You’re not what they call normal,
but you do have an episode of lunacy every now and then.

I’m not what they call sober,
for being drunk on you is the most beautiful intoxication.

We’re not what they call a couple,
since we can’t stand the unnecessary labelling.

But I do wish that we could unlearn
to take each other for granted.

And I do know this battle between us
is never-ending and ever-consuming.

But do I stop wishing for the moon
amidst the omniscient dark?

And do I stop hoping for a moment of serendipity
and a spoonful of romance?

You’re more than what you choose to reveal,
and it only pushes you to the edge.

I’m more than what I choose to hide,
and it only drives me one step closer to the cliff.

We’re more than what words allow us to express,
and sometimes they tear us apart.

But I do pretend to realise
that everything happens for a reason.

And I do know that sometimes
no reason is enough to be used as an excuse.

But do I stop dreaming about the warmth of sunrise
in your embrace?

And do I stop writing each time my heart bleeds
to write for you, for us?
